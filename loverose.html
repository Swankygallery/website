Love & Roses

In the quiet space between longing and letting go, there is love.
Not the kind shouted from rooftops, but the kind whispered into pillows,
traced across a cheek, left blooming in the shadow of absence.
These paintings hold that tenderness.

A rose does not ask to be remembered — it simply leaves its scent.
And so do we, when we love.

“She wears his absence like adornment. The echo of love never truly leaves.”
“She folds into herself, not to disappear but to feel every shade of what was.”
“Amid the storm, she kept the rose not for its petals, but for how it made her feel alive.”
“Breathing in deep the memory of what was.”
“She did not speak. The rose spoke for her, blooming from the place where longing lived.”
“Not every rose is peaceful. Some bloom from ache.”
“Neither goodbye nor forever, just love and safety in that moment.”
